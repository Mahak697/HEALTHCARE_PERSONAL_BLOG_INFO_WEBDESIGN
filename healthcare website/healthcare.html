    Acidity

    Acidity
    HeartBurn, pyrosis, cardialgia or acid indigestion

    Symptoms

    •A burning sensation in your chest (heartburn), usually after eating, which might be worse at night or while lying down.
    •Backwash (regurgitation) of food or sour liquid.
    •Upper abdominal or chest pain.
    •Trouble swallowing (dysphagia)
    •Sensation of a lump in your throat.

    Causes

    •It is a common medical condition caused due to excessive production of acid by the stomach, which flows back into the food pipe and causes pain or burning sensation in the lower chest area. According to nutritionists, poor food habits and an unhealthy lifestyle are responsible for acidity in individuals.

    Asthma

    Asthma
    bronchial asthma and chronic disease.

    Symptoms

    •Coughing, especially at night or in the morning.
    •Wheezing, a whistling sound when you breathe.
    •Shortness of breath.
    •Tightness, pain, or pressure in your chest.
    •Trouble sleeping because of breathing problems.

    Causes

    •The most common factors for developing asthma are having a parent with asthma, having a severe respiratory infection as a child, having an allergic condition, or being exposed to certain chemical irritants or industrial dusts in the workplace.

    Brain aneurysm

    Brain aneurysm
    cerebral aneurysm or an intracranial aneurysm

    Symptoms

    •Thunderclap headache (sudden onset and severe, often described as “the worst headache of my life”).
    •Nausea and vomiting.
    •Stiff neck.
    •Blurred or double vision.
    •Sensitivity to light (photophobia).
    •Seizures.
    •Drooping eyelid and a dilated pupil.
    •Pain above and behind your eye.

    Causes

    •Brain aneurysms are caused by a weakness in the walls of blood vessels in the brain. There are several reasons why this may happen, although an exact cause isn't always clear. The brain requires a large supply of blood delivered via the main blood vessels that run up the neck and into the brain.

    Cancer

    Cancer
    nodule, mass, or neoplasm.

    Symptoms

    •Fatigue or extreme tiredness that doesn't get better with rest.
    •Weight loss or gain of 10 pounds or more for no known reason.
    •Eating problems such as not feeling hungry, trouble swallowing, belly pain, or nausea and vomiting.
    •Swelling or lumps anywhere in the body.
    •Pain, especially new or with no known reason, that doesn't go away or gets worse.
    •Skin changes such as a lump that bleeds or turns scaly, a new mole or a change in a mole, a sore that does not heal, or a yellowish color to the skin or eyes (jaundice).
    •Unusual bleeding or bruising for no known reason.
    •Mouth changes such as sores, bleeding, pain, or numbness.

    Causes

    •physical carcinogens, such as ultraviolet and ionizing radiation.
    •chemical carcinogens, such as asbestos, components of tobacco smoke, alcohol, aflatoxin (a food contaminant),and arsenic (a drinking water contaminant).
    •biological carcinogens, such as infections from certain viruses, bacteria, or parasites.

    Diphtheria

    Diphtheria
    Syrian ulcer, membranous angina, malignant croup, and Boulogne sore throat.

    Symptoms

    •A thick, gray membrane covering the throat and tonsils.
    •A sore throat and hoarseness.
    •Swollen glands (enlarged lymph nodes) in the neck.
    •Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing.
    •Nasal discharge.
    •Fever and chills.
    •Tiredness

    Causes

    •Diphtheria is a serious infection caused by strains of bacteria called Corynebacterium diphtheriae that make a toxin. It is the toxin that can cause people to get very sick.

    Dry cough

    Dry cough
    nonproductive cough and post-viral cough.

    Symptoms

    •Dry Skin.
    •Chapped Lips.
    •Dry Eyes.
    •Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing.
    •Nasal Irritation.
    •Respiratory Issues.
    •Increased Susceptibility to Illness

    Causes

    •Geographic Location: Regions farther from large bodies of water, such as oceans or seas, tend to experience drier cold weather. Water bodies can moderate temperatures and provide moisture to the air.
    •Seasonal Changes: Certain seasons, especially winter, are often associated with drier and colder air. During winter, cold air from the polar regions can move into areas, reducing the overall humidity levels.
    •High Pressure Systems: High-pressure systems in the atmosphere are associated with clear skies and calm weather. These systems often result in dry and cold conditions. Cold air masses associated with high pressure can lack the moisture content found in warmer air.
    •Wind Patterns: Wind can influence humidity levels. For example, winds blowing from dry continental regions can bring dry and cold air to other areas.
    •Altitude: Higher elevations generally have lower humidity levels. In mountainous regions, the air can be both cold and dry due to the lower moisture content at higher altitudes.

    Ebola virus disease (EVD)

    Ebola virus disease (EVD)
    Ebola hemorrhagic fever

    Symptoms

    •Fever.
    •Aches and pains, such as severe headache and muscle and joint pain.
    •Weakness and fatigue.
    •Sore throat.
    •Loss of appetite.
    •Gastrointestinal symptoms including abdominal pain, diarrhea, and vomiting

    Causes

    •Ebola, previously known as Ebola hemorrhagic fever, is a rare and deadly disease caused by infection with one of the Ebola virus strains. Ebola can cause disease in humans and nonhuman primates (monkeys, gorillas, and chimpanzees). Ebola is caused by infection with a virus of the family Filoviridae, genus Ebolavirus.

    Food poisoning

    Food poisoning
    foodborne illness

    Symptoms

    •feeling sick (nausea)
    •diarrhoea.
    •being sick (vomiting)
    •stomach cramps.
    •a high temperature of 38C or above.
    •feeling generally unwell- such as feeling tired or having aches and chills.

    Causes

    •Infections with microbes—viruses, bacteria, and parasites—cause most food poisoning. Harmful chemicals also cause some cases of food poisoning. Microbes can spread to food at any time while the food is grown, harvested or slaughtered, processed, stored, shipped, or prepared.

    Gallstones

    Gallstones
    Cholelithiasis

    Symptoms

    •pain in your abdomen lasting several hours
    •nausea and vomiting
    •fever—even a low-grade fever—or chills
    •yellowish color of your skin or whites of your eyes, called jaundice
    •tea-colored urine and light-colored stools

    Causes

    •Gallstones form when bile stored in the gallbladder hardens into stone-like material. Too much cholesterol, bile salts, or bilirubin (bile pigment) can cause gallstones. When gallstones are present in the gallbladder itself, it is called cholelithiasis.

Stressful life events or trauma — such as the death or illness of a loved one, divorce, or a job loss — also may lead to insomnia.

    Thyroid

    Thyroid
    Glandula Thyreoidea

    Symptoms

    •Experiencing anxiety, irritability and nervousness.
    •Having trouble sleeping.
    •Losing weight.
    •Having an enlarged thyroid gland or a goiter.
    •Having muscle weakness and tremors.
    •Feeling sensitive to heat.
    •Having vision problems or eye irritation.
    •Experiencing irregular menstrual periods or having your menstrual cycle stop.

    Causes

    •Iodine deficiency:- Iodine is used by the thyroid to produce hormones.
    •A non-functioning thyroid gland:- Sometimes, the thyroid gland doesn't work correctly from birth. This affects about 1 in 4,000 newborns.
